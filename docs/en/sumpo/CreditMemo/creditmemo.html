CreditMemo User Guide

Payment memo for later account debits

📣
Known Issue The "lock" icon in the debit details has become invisible. You can still unlock it by tapping where it should be. This occurred on iOS 18–25. It is fixed in version 2.4.2 — please update to the latest version.

CreditMemo helps you track payments that will be debited from your bank account later. It covers credit-card spending, credit-linked wallet top-ups, N-days-later debits, fixed-date debits, and similar deferred payment methods. Immediate debit-card payments, cash top-ups, and other payments that settle right away are outside the scope.

Basic Flow

1 Record a Payment

Enter the amount, then add a label or tags when needed.

2 Choose a Method

Select the card or deferred payment method that determines the debit schedule.

3 Review Debits

Check unpaid and paid items, and review upcoming debits by account.

4 Review or Back Up

Edit history when needed, or export JSON data for backup.

⚠️ Data is not encrypted. Do not store personal or confidential information.

Entering a New Payment

Start by entering the amount and saving the record. You can fill in the payment method, label, and tags later. Beginner mode shows this workflow directly in the form.

Basic Fields

Amount Entered through the numeric keypad. This is required before saving.
Use Date Selected from the calendar. The later debit date is calculated automatically from the chosen payment method.
Payment Method Choose a card or other deferred method. You may also save it as unselected and fix it later from History.
Account Shown only when the selected payment method has no linked account yet.
Label A short clue such as a shop or purpose. Past labels are suggested as you type.

Optional Fields

Tags Available in Expert mode. You can assign more than one tag and change them later.
Repeat Available for lump-sum records in Expert mode. When the record is marked paid, the app can create the next monthly or periodic copy automatically.
Notes Free-form notes for anything you want to keep with the record.

Similar Records

Copy from existing From History, pick a matching-amount record or a recently edited record and copy it into a new payment. You can also copy from Pay Methods, or copy the debit date + method from Debit Status.

Debit Date — Automatic / Manual

Auto The debit date is computed automatically from the use date and the payment method (closing day / payment day / payment month, or N-days-later type). It updates automatically when you change the use date or payment method. In Japan locale, with the setting on, dates that fall on a weekend or national holiday are shifted to the next business day.
Manual Tap the icon to fix the date at that moment. From then on, changing the use date or payment method does not move the debit date. Useful for shifting a payment that missed a cutoff into the next debit cycle. Tap the date to pick a date from the calendar, or use the "Prev / Next due date" buttons to jump by one payment cycle.
Fix to Manual when paid When you mark an item as paid, its debit date automatically switches to Manual and is fixed so it doesn't drift when you change the use date or payment method later. The fixed state stays even if you move the item back to unpaid, but you can tap the icon to switch back to Auto.
2-installment payment The debit date is shown on two rows (1st and 2nd installment), and the Auto / Manual icon toggles both rows together. The amount is split automatically; tap an amount to redistribute manually within the range of 1 to (total − 1), with the other side updating accordingly. The 2nd installment's date is constrained to fall after the 1st; if you move the 1st past the 2nd, the app shifts the 2nd to the following month automatically. Once the 1st installment is marked as paid, fields other than the 2nd installment's date (amount, use date, payment method, etc.) become read-only. Enable "Use 2-installment payment" in Settings to make this option available for new entries — existing 2-installment records remain visible and editable even when the setting is off.

Reviewing History

Order Sort by date or amount. Each sort can be toggled between descending and ascending order.
Filters Choose the period from 3 months, 1 year, 3 years, or all time. You can also filter by all, has blanks, payment method, account, or multiple tags. Has Blanks includes records missing a payment method or label.
Editing Open any row to edit or delete it. Once a record is already fully paid, some core fields such as amount, date, and payment method are locked.

Debit Status

Debit Status combines upcoming debits and already-paid ones in one flow. When you confirm a bank debit, use the arrow icon to move that account group to Paid. You can move it back to unpaid whenever needed.
Unpaid Sections Unpaid items are separated into upcoming and overdue groups. If overdue items exist, a segmented switch appears.
Recent Debit Total The top menu shows the total for your configured debit range. You can choose 1-20 days or 1 month.
Grouped by Account Even on the same debit date, items linked to different accounts are handled as separate debit groups. You can switch grouping by date, payment method, or account.
Filters Choose all, payment method, or account. When you pick a method or account, the grouping switches to date so the timeline is easier to read.
Details Open a debit row to inspect the payment-method sections and original payment records included in that debit.
Item-level adjustment In Debit Details, you can move a single item between unpaid and paid. Use this when only one near-cutoff item is debited next time.
Lock Toggle Debit Details also include a lock toggle for each item. This restores the old app's confirmation check feature, but uses a lock icon instead of a checkmark. A locked item means you have already verified it against your statement or passbook.

Pay Methods, Accounts, and Tags

Pay Methods

Scope Register only payment methods that will debit your bank account later. For PayPay, credit-linked use and wallet top-up count, but spending already topped-up balance does not.
Schedule Types Choose either Closing Day / Payment Day or N Days Later. Month-end can be used as the closing or payment day.
Linked Account Each pay method can link to one account. Changing that link rebuilds the related historical invoice and debit grouping so old account groups do not remain behind.
Open to Edit Tapping a row in the Pay Methods list opens the edit screen. Status review is handled from Debit Status grouping and filters.

Accounts and Tags

Accounts Store the names of debit destination accounts. You can also start from built-in presets.
Tags Each tag can show the list of related payment records for review and cleanup.

Backup and Settings

JSON Backup

Export Export all data as a JSON file. Choose Compact for storage or Pretty for easier inspection.
Import Import an exported JSON file. Re-importing the same data updates records without creating duplicates. Invoice and debit paid states are restored as well.
Pruning Old Data If the database grows too large, you can prune older records from Settings. In practice, around three years of history is the intended working range.

Basic Specs

Target Payments Payments debited from your account on a later date (instant-debit cards and cash top-ups are out of scope)
Platform iOS 18 or later
Maximum Amount 99,999,999
Date Range 2000-01-01 to 2100-12-31
Label Length Up to 50 characters
Note Length Up to 200 characters
Payment Methods Lump sum or 2-payment installment (interest-free, fee-free assumed). Three or more installments and bonus payments are not supported
Debit Date Calculation Computed automatically from closing day / payment month / payment day. N-days-later cards use use-date + N days. When no payment method is selected, the debit date equals the use date
Weekend/Holiday Shift Japan locale only. With the setting on (default), closing/payment-day cards have their debit date shifted to the next business day when it falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or Japanese national holiday. Covers holidays from the 1948 law to 2099, including special transfers, substitute holidays, and national holidays
Debit Date Lock Switch each item between manual and automatic. Items marked paid are locked automatically
Repeat 1 to 99 months. When marked paid, a copy is generated automatically for the next cycle
Tags Multiple tags per item. Used for filtering and summary in lists
Debit Total Period Recent 1 to 20 days, 30, 35, 40, 50, or 60 days (default 15 days)
Data Storage On-device only (SwiftData). No cloud sync. You can back up the device itself via iCloud Backup
Backup / Restore Export / import the entire dataset as JSON from Settings (compact or pretty format)
JSON Import Policy Merge (upsert) by id. Restores masters (accounts, methods, tags), records, paid/unpaid state, and debit-date locks. Missing array keys are treated as unspecified and skipped. If a save fails partway, all changes are rolled back so the database stays consistent
JSON Import Normalization Payment method is normalized to 1 (lump sum) or 2 (2-payment); anything else becomes lump sum. For N-days-later cards, payment month is normalized to 0. Manually adjusted amounts in a 2-payment record are preserved on import. Amounts that fail to parse are imported as 0 and a warning is logged
Legacy Migration Auto-migrates from the legacy Core Data file (AzCredit.sqlite). On failure, the next launch retries automatically. Missing WAL/SHM after iCloud restore is auto-repaired
Prune Old Records Settings can bulk-delete records older than a chosen number of years
Privacy No data is sent to any external server. The local database is not encrypted, so the app is designed not to store sensitive personal information
